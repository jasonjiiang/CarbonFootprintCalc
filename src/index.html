FAQ

(Frequently Asked Questions)

What is a carbon footprint?

A Carbon footprint is the measure of Carbon Dioxide an individual has released into the atmosphere due to their actions. This can include but is not limited to Transportation, Electricity, Recycling.

How does the carbon footprint calculator work?

The carbon footprint calculator is used to calculate an individual's total yearly carbon emissions by using algorithms to calculate emissions from Home, Transport and Recycling.

Why do I need to calculate my carbon footprint?

Whether you’re an individual or a business, calculating your carbon footprint can be useful as it can make you aware of the impact you have on the environment and where to reduce usage to prevent waste and get closer to becoming carbon neutral

What factors contribute to my carbon footprint?

The most common factors that contribute to carbon emissions are: Energy Consumption, Transportation, Diet, Housing, Consumer goods and Waste. However, there are more factors but are much harder to calculate

Why is reducing carbon emissions important?

Reducing carbon emissions helps in slowing climate change which helps in slowing the rate of temperature rise, ocean acidification and ice caps melting. It can also improve public health as it reduces air pollution.

How much carbon emissions should I reduce to help make an impact?

By 2030 the average emissions per person should reduce by 2-2.5tonnes per year to preserve a liveable climate
